
# Recruit

Not everyone has the dexterity or mental capacity for tech, but you can't know that right away. Give them a chance to prove themselves, to learn, and to grow. I can't count the number of times I would be pleasantly surprised by how much some volunteers would learn. Aim to recruit new members, no matter how young or old.

---
# Develop

You can start training someone by having them shadow someone (letting them observe first) or on-the-job-training (OJT) where they take on the role while someone will guide them. Shadowing allows the trainee to learn the role through the best practices and without being pressured. OJT is a 'trial by fire' approach where they are put under pressure while being coached. 

I personally prefer the OJT approach for training because it gives them the opportunity to make mistakes while there is someone to guide them. It also shows me how well they handle pressure. However, I only use this approach if the one guiding them is myself, or someone with plenty of experience.

- The general rule of thumb is to have them shadow/OJT at least 2-3 times.
- A TD trainee needs to be familiar with both [[Spreadsheet#^02dfdc|SC and SM roles.]]
- Don't be afraid to address bad habits.
- Always encourage.
- Be an example of excellence.

# Empower
<br>
<div style="font-family: monospace; font-size: 16px; background-color: #1e1e1e; color: #d4d4d4; padding: 12px; border-radius: 6px; overflow-x: auto;">
In Judges chapter 7, Gideon needed men for his army. God chose 300 out of 10,000 men (3%) to be Israel's fighers. How were they chosen? Based on how they drank water. The men who didn't let their guard down, even when they should be resting, were the ones chosen. 
<br>
<br>
Likewise, I want you to look for the smallest qualities in each member that sets them apart. Look at the minute details which often go overlooked: how they are always alert, how they handle being corrected, how they always come in early, how they eagerly want to learn more, how they commit to serve even if it's inconvenient to their schedule.
<br>
<br>
They may be few, but it's up to God to use them mightily. Don't go searching for the "perfect volunteer" because there is none. But look for the volunteer who strives for excellence.
</div>

<br>
Some volunteers tend to be comfortable with a single role. Don't be afraid to take them outside of their comfort zone by putting them in new roles or handle new tasks.

Keep an eye out for those with the skill, initiative, and passion for tech. Further develop and empower them for [[index#^1bf68c|leadership and management roles]].
